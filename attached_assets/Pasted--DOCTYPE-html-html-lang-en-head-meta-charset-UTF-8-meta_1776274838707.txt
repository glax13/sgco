    <h1 class="hpos-hl">The High Performance<br>Operating System</h1>
    <div class="hpos-two">
      <div class="hpos-copy">
        <p>Performance is not created by programmes. It is emergent from system coherence — from the interaction between strategy, governance, people, pathways, data, and culture across the whole organisation.</p>
        <p>The HPOS is the framework I use to see, diagnose, and redesign how performance is actually produced. Not a model you install. A lens applied at board level, where the structural decisions are made.</p>
        <p>It is directly applicable to elite sport organisations, enterprise governance design, and AI adoption — anywhere that structural alignment determines whether outcomes land where the strategy intended.</p>
        <p class="note">Performance Debt is the diagnostic concept within the HPOS — the structural accumulation of misalignment across cycles, until someone absorbs the cost.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="def-box">
        <div class="def-tag">System Definition</div>
        <div class="def-title">High Performance Operating System</div>
        <div class="def-body">A system-level construct describing how strategy, governance, people, pathways, data, and culture interact to produce outcomes. Performance is emergent from coherence between these dimensions — not from any one in isolation.</div>
        <div class="def-note">Applicable wherever structural alignment determines outcome quality — elite sport, enterprise transformation, AI governance, and GRC design.</div>
      </div>
